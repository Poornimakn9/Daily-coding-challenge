**Day 19 – Farm Yield Optimization (SQL Project)**
**Overview**
This project focuses on analyzing agricultural data to improve crop yield and optimize resource usage. Using SQL, we explore how factors like irrigation, soil type, and weather conditions impact farm productivity.

**Database Schema**

The project is based on four tables:

farmers → Farmer details
plots → Plot information (crop type, soil type)
yields → Harvest data (yield, weather conditions)
irrigation_logs → Water usage records
**Objectives**
Analyze farm productivity
Optimize water consumption
Understand environmental impact on crops
Improve farmer resource management
Perform advanced yield vs water efficiency analysis
**Tasks & Solutions**
✅ 1. Productivity & Performance
Identified Top 3 most productive plots based on average yield
Calculated total water consumption per plot and ranked them
✅ 2. Yield & Environmental Analysis
Analyzed average yield by crop type and weather condition
Found highest-yielding plot for each soil type
✅ 3. Farmer & Resource Management
Identified farmer with lowest average water consumption
Calculated monthly harvest trends (last 12 months)
✅ 4. Advanced Analysis (Bonus)
Detected inefficient plots:
Below-average yield
Above-average water usage
**Key Insights**
Some plots produce consistently higher yields due to favorable soil conditions
Water usage varies significantly across plots
Weather conditions directly influence crop performance
Certain plots use more water but still produce low yield (inefficiency)
