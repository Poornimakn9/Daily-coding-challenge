**Day 13 – Stress Level Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)**
** Project Overview**
This project focuses on analyzing employee stress levels using lifestyle and work-related data such as working hours, sleep patterns, exercise habits, and caffeine intake.

The objective is to identify key factors contributing to stress and provide actionable insights to improve employee well-being and productivity.

**Key KPIs**
Average Stress Level across organization
Percentage of Employees with High Stress (≥ 4)
Average Sleep Hours vs Work Hours
Sleep-to-Work Ratio

**DAX Measures Used**
High Stress %
Avg Stress Level
Avg Sleep Hours
Avg Work Hours
Sleep Work Ratio

**Dashboard Visuals**
Column Chart: Stress Level by Department
Line Chart: Stress Trend Over Time
Scatter Plot: Work Hours vs Stress Level (with trend line)
Donut Chart: Stress Level Distribution
KPI Cards for key metrics
Conditional formatting to highlight high-risk employees

**Key Insights**
Finance and IT departments show the highest average stress levels, indicating workload pressure.
Employees work longer hours (8.5+ hrs), which strongly correlates with higher stress.
Average sleep (≈6 hrs) is low, and less sleep leads to extreme stress levels.
Over 80% of employees fall under medium to high stress, showing a widespread issue.
Around 123 employees are in high-stress category, requiring immediate attention.
Stress levels fluctuate over time, suggesting periodic workload spikes or deadlines.

**Suggestion:**

Introduce work-life balance policies
Encourage sleep & wellness programs
Monitor high-risk employees proactively

Power BI

Data Visualization Techniques
